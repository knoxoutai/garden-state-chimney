Vented vs. Vent-Free Gas Logs

There are two main types of gas logs, and the right choice depends on your fireplace and what you want:

  • Vented gas logs produce the most realistic, full flame and are used with the chimney damper open. They prioritize ambiance over heat output.
  • Vent-free gas logs burn very efficiently and put more heat into the room, operating with the damper closed. They have specific room-size and clearance requirements.

We’ll evaluate your fireplace and help you pick the option that fits your space, your local code, and how you plan to use it.

Why Professional Installation Matters

Gas logs involve a gas connection, proper burner placement, and correct log positioning — placement that’s off can cause sooting, poor flames, or unsafe operation. Our certified team installs your set correctly, tests it, and shows you how to operate it safely.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between vented and vent-free gas logs?
Vented logs give the most realistic flame and run with the damper open, prioritizing looks. Vent-free logs burn efficiently and add more heat with the damper closed, but have room-size and clearance rules. We'll help you choose.
Can I put gas logs in my existing wood-burning fireplace?
Usually yes, provided your fireplace and gas supply are suitable. We'll inspect your firebox and chimney and recommend the right vented or vent-free set.
Are gas logs safe?
When properly installed and maintained by a certified professional, yes. Correct burner and log placement plus annual service keep them operating safely. Improper DIY installation is where problems arise.
How much does gas log installation cost?
It depends on the log set you choose and your fireplace setup. We don't post prices online, but we'll provide a clear estimate. Call 973-519-0802 to talk it through.
