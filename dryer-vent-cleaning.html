Why Dryer Vent Cleaning Matters

According to the U.S. Fire Administration, thousands of home fires each year are caused by clothes dryers — and failure to clean them is the leading factor. Over time, lint escapes the lint trap and accumulates inside the vent duct. That buildup traps heat and provides fuel for a fire.

Signs Your Dryer Vent Needs Cleaning

  • Clothes take longer than one cycle to dry
  • The dryer or laundry room feels unusually hot
  • A burning or musty smell during operation
  • The lint flap on the outside of your home barely opens
  • It’s been more than a year since your last cleaning

How Often Should a Dryer Vent Be Cleaned?

For most households, once a year is the right cadence. If you have a large family, do frequent loads, or have a long vent run, you may benefit from more frequent service. We’ll give you an honest recommendation based on your setup.

What Our Service Includes

We remove lint and debris from the full length of the vent duct, check the exterior vent flap, confirm proper airflow, and make sure everything is reconnected and venting safely — leaving your laundry area cleaner than we found it.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does dryer vent cleaning take?
Most standard dryer vent cleanings take 30 to 60 minutes, depending on the length and configuration of your vent run.
Is a dirty dryer vent really a fire risk?
Yes. Lint is highly flammable, and a clogged vent traps heat. Failure to clean dryer vents is the leading cause of clothes-dryer fires, which is why annual cleaning is strongly recommended.
Do you clean vents for all dryer types?
Yes, we service both gas and electric dryer vents, including longer and rooftop vent runs common in many New Jersey homes.
