Why a Professional Gas Fireplace Installation Matters

Gas appliances involve fuel lines, venting, and ignition systems — all of which must be installed precisely. A poor installation risks gas leaks, carbon monoxide, and fire. As certified hearth professionals, we ensure your gas fireplace is installed correctly, vented properly, and tested before we leave.

Gas Fireplace Options We Install

  • Gas fireplace inserts: Convert an existing masonry fireplace into an efficient, clean-burning gas unit.
  • Built-in gas fireplaces: Add a brand-new fireplace to nearly any room.
  • Direct-vent & ventless models: We’ll recommend the right venting type for your home and goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I convert my wood-burning fireplace to gas?
In most cases, yes. A gas insert is a popular way to convert an existing masonry fireplace into a cleaner, more efficient gas unit. We'll assess your fireplace and recommend the best option.
What's the difference between direct-vent and ventless gas fireplaces?
Direct-vent models exhaust combustion byproducts outside through a sealed vent, while ventless models burn very cleanly and require no vent. We'll help you choose the right type for your home and local code.
Do you handle the gas line connection?
Yes. Our certified team handles the appliance installation and venting. Where a new or modified gas supply line is needed, we coordinate the work so everything is safe and to code.
How much does gas fireplace installation cost?
It depends on the unit, venting, and any gas-line work. We don't post prices online, but we'll give you a clear estimate. Call 973-519-0802 to discuss your project.
